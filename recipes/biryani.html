Chicken Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Biryani

  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • A pinch of saffron (optional)
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala

Instructions

Marinating the Chicken

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Mix well to coat the chicken evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight for best results.

Preparing the Rice

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, bay leaf, and salt.
  3. Add the rinsed rice and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (it should still have a bite to it). Drain and set aside.

Making the Biryani

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot. Add the sliced onions and fry until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish.
  2. To the remaining onions in the pot, add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.
  3. Add the marinated chicken and cook on medium heat until the chicken is cooked through and the oil starts to separate again.
  4. Add the biryani masala and mix well.
  5. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle the chopped cilantro, mint leaves, and the reserved fried onions on top.
  6. In a small bowl, warm the milk and add the saffron strands. Pour this saffron milk over the rice. (If not using saffron, you can add a few drops of yellow food coloring to the milk instead.)
  7. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the lid with dough to ensure no steam escapes. Cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Serving

  1. Once the biryani is done, gently fluff up the rice with a fork, mixing the layers slightly.
  2. Serve hot with raita, a side salad, or any other accompaniment of your choice.
